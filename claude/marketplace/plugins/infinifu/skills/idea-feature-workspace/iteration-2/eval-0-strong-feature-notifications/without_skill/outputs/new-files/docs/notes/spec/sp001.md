## problem

We have three services (`auth`, `metrics`, `reports`) and each one
carries its own copy/pasted `smtplib` snippet to send email:

- `src/services/auth/__init__.py` — welcome emails via direct `mail`
  shell-out.
- `src/services/metrics/__init__.py` — alert emails via ad-hoc
  `smtplib`, mixed with stdout alerts.
- `src/services/reports/__init__.py` — report-ready CSV emails via
  `smtplib`.

This is exactly the drift [[adr0003]] flagged as a known
consequence: "three copy/paste smtplib snippets across services …
any change to MTA host requires editing three places … no metrics on
send rate, bounces, or latency." [[adr0003]] is now the explicit
constraint we want to revisit at spec stage.

Two new pressures push it past acceptable:

1. The metrics team wants **Slack alerts** for daytime incidents — a
   second channel, not just email. Today there is no shared place to
   add it; copy/pasting a second snippet across three services
   compounds the drift.
2. The metrics team also wants **pager-style SMS** for after-hours
   alerts. SMS is not committed for the first delivery but must be a
   plausible extension — the capability shape chosen now should not
   foreclose a third channel.

The analyst persona [[pn001]] also lists "get notified when
long-running reports finish" as a primary goal that is currently
served by the reports service's ad-hoc smtplib. Captured as a fresh
story [[us004]] so the spec has a concrete backlog item that benefits
from the consolidation rather than only being an internal refactor.

**Scope of this idea capture (idea stage only).** This spec captures
the *problem* — the consolidation case, the consumers, the channel
matrix, and the [[adr0003]] tension that has to be revisited.
Choosing the API shape (sync vs queued), the slack/sms libraries,
the templating story, the retry/dedup behavior, and minting the new
`ft###` Feature are **explicit non-goals here** — those land at spec
stage when this `sp001` moves from `idea` to `spec`.

**Known consumers and what they want from a shared capability.**

| Service   | Channels today | Channels wanted          | Volume / urgency       |
|-----------|----------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| auth      | email          | email                    | low, transactional     |
| metrics   | email, stdout  | email + slack (now), sms (later, after-hours) | bursty, urgent |
| reports   | email          | email + slack (later)    | per-job, non-urgent    |

**Non-goals at idea stage (revisit at spec).**

- Templating / formatting engine choice.
- Sync vs async / queued delivery.
- Retry, dedup, and bounce handling policy.
- SMS provider selection (Twilio / etc.) and on-call routing.
- Per-user notification preferences storage.
- Whether to keep auth's welcome-email path in scope of the first
  cut or carve it out as a follow-up.

**Categories.** `[[cat003|infrastructure]]` (cross-service plumbing
is the dominant axis) and `[[cat004|observability]]` (metrics team's
alert channels are the load-bearing driver). [[cat001|security]] is
*not* claimed at idea stage — credentials/secrets handling will
surface at spec stage and may pull cat001 in, but it is not the
defining bucket today.

**ADR tension to resolve at spec stage.** [[adr0003]] currently
mandates "services use smtplib directly, no relay." Minting a shared
notifications feature with multiple channels supersedes that
decision; the spec stage owes a new ADR superseding [[adr0003]] with
"services route notifications through ft### shared capability."
