## problem
Legal asks the compliance officer ([[pn003]]) every quarter for proof
that the system actually executed its data-deletion policy. Today
adr0002 says "reports retained 90 days; hard-deleted by nightly job"
but nothing records *which* rows were deleted, *when*, or *by which
job run*. The retention job is essentially silent — once a row is
gone, there is no defensible trace.

The compliance officer needs a per-quarter CSV listing every
hard-deleted row (table, primary key, deletion timestamp, triggering
job id), tamper-evident via a detached signature from a
vault-managed key. The export must be reproducible (same quarter,
same content), the signature verifiable without re-running the
exporter, and the underlying ledger must be append-only so a future
quarter cannot rewrite an earlier one.

Out of scope for this spec: SSO/SAML for the compliance officer
(adr0001 keeps basic-auth as the only option), email delivery of the
artefact (adr0003 / no shared notification layer — first quarter is
out-of-band hand-off), and historical back-fill of deletions that
happened before the audit-log lands.
