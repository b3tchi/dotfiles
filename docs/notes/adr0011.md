# ADR [[cat001]] [[cat002]] [[product]]

## title
bespoke clipnotify file store over vendor clipboard managers

## context
[[ft007]] (shared-clipboard-history) needs a backend with four narrow
properties: text-only capture on X11, memory-only history, deterministic
newest-first listing, and byte-exact retrieval. Three vendor choices were
made and unmade on this surface inside two days:

**copyq 16.0.0** shipped with [[sp014]]. It met every functional bar but is
a Qt6 GUI application pressed into headless service: 96 linked libraries,
qtkeychain/qca/scripting-engine attack surface on a secrets-adjacent
daemon, and a Qt chain that forecloses Termux/proot. [[sp014]] deliberately
declined to record the backend choice as an ADR, keeping it in
`## solution`. **That was wrong**: a vendor choice on a surface this
sensitive is exactly what ADRs are for, and its absence meant the swap
one spec later re-derived the constraints from scratch.

**clipcat 0.25.0** was validated by [[poc010]] (secret-atom gate stronger
than copyq's, 4 linked libs, 9.9 MiB private RSS) and adopted by [[sp016]]
— then **falsified in execution** by three upstream defects unresolvable
at the `clipcatctl` surface: `dotfiles-8il` (list order rides randomized
HashMap iteration — not newest-first, not even stable across calls),
`dotfiles-i9i` (`get` irreversibly escapes embedded control chars — a
literal `\n` substring and a real newline render byte-identical), and
`dotfiles-apl` (~25% of daemon starts go permanently deaf on an unguarded
`GetProperty` → `BadAtom`; only a process restart clears it, and nothing
observable says it happened).

**clipse v1.2.1** was evaluated as the fallback and rejected by
[[poc011]]: its listener startup SIGKILLs every process whose short name
is a *substring* of its watcher names (a backwards containment check) —
including `st`, the meta-wsl-i3 terminal — and it leaks entries to
persistent disk regardless of config, misclassifying plain text as
phantom PNGs.

[[poc012]] then validated the alternative: a `clipnotify`-blocked
`#!/bin/sh` loop writing sequence-numbered files under
`$XDG_RUNTIME_DIR`, no daemon at all. One correction surfaced while
building the real thing (dotfiles-egm.6): the poc's literal
`while clipnotify; do handle; done` shape is deaf for the whole of the
handler — its single fast read merely hid the gap; at production read
cost a 50 ms-spaced copy was measurably lost. The shipped loop pre-spawns
the next watcher before handling the current event, shrinking the
unsubscribed window to clipnotify startup (~ms). The poc zettel stays
immutable; this ADR is the record of that falsification.

## decision
[[ft007]]'s backend is a **bespoke clipnotify + file-store loop we own**
(`i3/scripts/clip-store.sh`, ~150 lines of `#!/bin/sh` per [[adr0002]]),
not a general-purpose clipboard manager. The hard clipboard problems stay
delegated to single-purpose tools from official repos — `clipnotify`
(XFixes event watching) and `xclip` (target negotiation, INCR) — and the
glue that remains is exactly the part vendors kept getting wrong:
ordering (monotonic seq filenames — deterministic by construction),
byte-exactness (`get` is `cat`), liveness (no daemon to go silently deaf;
a dead loop is `pgrep`-visible and restarted by i3 reload), and
memory-only (a tmpfs directory — a filesystem property, not config
discipline or a daemon promise).

The secret gate moves *into our loop*: the TARGETS pre-check + TOCTOU
re-check (both hint-atom spellings) is the same adversarially-tested code
on every capture path, instead of per-vendor config with per-vendor
bypass holes.

Vendor clipboard managers are foreclosed on this surface unless one
demonstrably clears the four properties above *at its CLI/IPC surface* —
in-process behavior that its interface escapes or reorders (i9i, 8il)
counts as failing. Rejected backends stay in-tree, links removed, as
rollback layers for one cycle: `copyq/` and `clipcat/`.

Vendor-choice decisions on secrets-adjacent surfaces get an ADR at the
spec that makes them — the [[sp014]] omission is not to be repeated.

## consequences
We own the loop's bugs forever; there is no upstream to file against. The
adversarial suites (71 assertions on the store alone, mutation-tested,
negative-controlled) are the warranty, and the surface is small enough
that they can be. Against that: eight upstream defects across two
evaluated vendors showed general-purpose managers are where the bugs
live, and every one of them was invisible until adversarial testing here
surfaced it.

The footprint argument dissolves rather than improves: no resident
backend at all beyond a blocked clipnotify (~1 MiB), vs copyq's 16 MiB
private / 96 libs. The Qt-chain blocker on Termux/proot is gone, though
extending [[ft007]] there remains unshipped scope.

Known costs accepted: a plaintext store exists on tmpfs for the session
lifetime and survives SIGKILL (same exposure as both prior backends); a
copy burst faster than one read coalesces to the selection's newest state
(not provokable at 50 ms spacing with the pre-spawned watcher); and this
choice ships **no security improvement** — the gate only fires on hint
atoms no local password manager emits; `dotfiles-cyg` / [[sp015]] remain
the actual fix.

This forecloses "just install a clipboard manager" as a future
convenience. Reopening it means re-litigating the four properties against
the evidence chain [[poc010]] → [[poc011]] → [[poc012]] recorded here.
